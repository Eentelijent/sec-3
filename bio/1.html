I. Introduction

Cells are the basic structures and function unit of life.
They have all the characteristics of life.

Cells can exist in the following ways:
  • Singly as independent uni-cellular organisms
  • As part of multi-cellular organisms

Longitudinal and transverse section


Longitudinal — cutting along the length of the cell.
Transverse — cutting across the length of the cell.
Transverse cutting is always perpendicular to the length of the cell.

II. Cell structures and organelles

A cell is made out of living material called protoplasm.

A living cell is made of three main parts:
  • Cell surface membrane (CSM)
  • Cytoplasm
  • Nucleus

Note:

  • When drawing the nucleus, draw some lines to represent the chromatin threads. Do not leave the nucleus empty!

  • Label the cytoplasm, nucleus, and CSM together so that they can be classified together as the protoplasm. (important!)

Protoplasm

2.1 Cell surface membrane/plasma membrane

Structure

  1. Partially-permeable membrane
  2. Made of proteins and lipids

Function

  1. Controls the movements of substances entering and leaving the cells
  2. Seperates the internal environment (cytoplasm) from its surroundings

2.2 Cytoplasm

Structure

  1. Collidial and viscous (jelly-like)
  2. Mainly water, with salts and sugars

Function

  1. Fluid that contains many small structures called organelles
    • Organelle: a sub-unit of a cell with a specialised function
  2. Region where many chemical reactions take place

2.3 Nucleus

Structure

  1. Filled with nucleoplasm
  2. Surrounded by a double membrane

2.4 Vacuoles

Structure

Generally a fluid-filled membrane with a single membrane.

In animal cells:
  • Many, Small, temporary vacuoles
In plant cells:
  • Single, large, central vacuole enclosed by a Partially-permeable membrane called a tonoplast
  • Cell sap is found within vacuole

Function

  • Stores substances (water and nutrients)
  • Collects and removes waste and harmful substances
In animal cells:
  • Stores water and food substances
In plant cells:
  • Cell sap stores dissolved substances such as sugars, mineral salts and amino acids
    • Keeps the cytoplasm concentration constant
    • Maintains turgor to keep the cell turgid and support the plant cell
