I. Introduction

Cells are the basic structures and function unit of life.
They have all the characteristics of life.

Cells can exist in the following ways:
  • Singly as independent uni-cellular organisms
  • As part of multi-cellular organisms

1.1   Light and electron microscopes

Difference between light and electron microscopes
Light microscope Electron microscope
Can magnify up to 1000x Can magnify up to 200,000x
Lower resolution Higher resolution
Photomicrographs can be coloured via staining Micrographs are black-and-white but can be artifically coloured
Can view the following:
  • Chloroplasts
  • Cell surface membrane
  • Cell wall
  • Cytoplasm
  • Cell vacuoles
  • Nucleus
 Can view the following, including those that can be viewed by light microscopes:
  • Mitochondria
  • Endoplasmic recticulum
  • Ribosomes
  • Golgi body
